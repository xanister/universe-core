## 🕮 *A Summary of the Lex Gaardium*

*As Set Down by Magister Jovaen Vallace, Archivist of the Crown-Law Chamber, Year 1453 of the Fourth Age*

> *“Though the crown wavers, and the sea encroaches, still the Lex binds us. It is the spine of Gaardia.”*
> — High Lexic Aumyr Torcalen, 1232 4A

---

### I. On the Origins of the Lex

The **Lex Gaardium**—formally *Corpus Juris Gaardianum*—was first codified under **King Deremond II**, reigning from 215 to 237 4A. Drawn from fragmentary Imperial edicts, noble compacts, dwarven arbitration rituals, and the oral oaths of the Rift-road courts, it was compiled by **Lexic Myrrin Haar**, who famously described it as “binding blood with ink.”

The Lex was amended substantially following the **Duras Insurrection (312 4A)**, the **Crater Exile Crisis (678 4A)**, and most recently after the **Seuran Maritime Accords (1411 4A)**.

---

### II. On the Nature of Authority

The Lex affirms:

* That sovereignty flows from *Tharianic inheritance*, but must be *ratified by lawful counsel*.
* That the **Crown possesses supreme right over law**, but is itself bound to the Lex unless it invokes the **Clause of Ash** (emergency dominion).
* That the **Privy Synod** may challenge a monarch’s edict if it contradicts the foundational rights (“the Five Graces”) enshrined by the Charter of Queen Iridelle IV.

---

### III. The Five Graces of the Law

These sacred protections form the cornerstone of Gaardian civic identity:

1. **The Grace of Shelter** – No citizen may be evicted from roof or burrow without warrant.
2. **The Grace of Challenge** – All nobles may contest judgments through the Shield Trial, unless exiled or undead.
3. **The Grace of Silence** – No one may be compelled to speak under magic save in matters of necromancy.
4. **The Grace of Inheritance** – Goods and titles may not be seized without formal writ of lineal failure.
5. **The Grace of Bread** – During famine or siege, grain stores must be opened to the peasantry on the 7th day.

Violations of the Graces are known as **Lex-Breachings**, and carry severe penalties—typically exile, dismemberment, or conscription into the Crater Watch.

---

### IV. On Magic and Its Bounds

The Lex strictly regulates magic through the **Mage-Writ Statutes**:

* No sorcery may be practiced in court without a Crown Seal.

---

### V. On Trade, Salt, and Maritime Law

* All merchant vessels entering Gaardian waters must register cargo with the **Salt Guild**, as mandated by the **Salt-Tithe Edict of 272 4A**.
* Piracy, including Seuran freebooting, is punished by ship seizure and public oarbinding.
* The **Duras Exemption** grants Duke Henril’s house the right to levy internal tariffs—this remains a point of dispute since 1407.

---

### VI. On Judgment and Courts

There are five tiers of legal forum:

1. **The Hearth-Tier** – Local magistrates for common disputes.
2. **The Guild-Courts** – Commercial and labor grievances; often presided over by trade-clerks.
3. **The Circuit Courts** – Overseen by **Roving Arbiters** who carry the King's Writ.
4. **The Synod Tribunals** – Reserved for cases involving noble houses or faith orders.
5. **The High Seat of the Crown** – Final arbiter of all matters; rarely convened.

Verdicts may be appealed once, unless declared “Doom-Sealed” by a Synod vote.

---

### VII. On Recent Amendments

As of the current year (1453 4A), several amendments have been made or proposed:

* **The Witch-Recompense Decree (1441 4A):** Grants pardon to hedge-practitioners aiding rural health during plague years.
* **The Crater Border Edict (1448 4A):** Defines the Crater as a legal non-jurisdiction—no bounty law applies within its bounds.
* **The Silverblood Bill (1452 4A):** Proposed but unratified—would bar undead persons from inheritance outside of Kensir.

---

### VIII. Final Reflection

Though often dismissed as arcane, overly symbolic, or contradictory, the **Lex Gaardium** remains a living pillar of Gaardian society. As the continent writhes beneath unstable crowns and fading alliances, it is the Lex—not the sword—that binds the realm to memory and meaning.

> *“For as long as oaths are etched and justice rides with seal and lantern, the realm endures.”*
> —Magister Vallace, concluding address to the Hall of Inkscribes, High Gaardia, 1453 4A
