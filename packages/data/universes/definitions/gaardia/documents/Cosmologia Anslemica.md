### **Cosmologia Anslemica: A Treatise on the Known Universe (circa 1430s 4th Age)**

### 

### **Preface**

In the course of my lifelong pursuit of understanding our vast cosmos, I have come to appreciate the delicate balance of knowledge and mystery that defines our existence. Our realm, Anslem, bears the marks of profound celestial and subterranean events—none more telling than the great Crater, which predates the tragic Fall of Illuminus by several years, a stark reminder of our vulnerability in the face of cosmic upheaval. The subsequent creation of the Rift, resulting directly from the catastrophic descent of Illuminus, reshaped not only the physical landscape but also our very comprehension of the world we inhabit. It is within this context, both historical and mystical, that I offer this treatise, hoping to illuminate the shadows of our understanding and inspire further inquiry.

### **Introduction: Contextualizing cosmological thought in Gaardia**

### **Chapter I: The Uppermost Realm—Ahm'a, the Kingdom Above Air**

Ahm’a, traditionally regarded as a domain of myth and religious symbolism, has recently become a topic of scholarly inquiry and empirical observation. Through the advancements of the Rift-spyglass, scholars have been able to glean unprecedented insights into this elusive realm. High above Anslem's atmosphere, Ahm'a consists of immense floating landmasses, often referred to colloquially as “floating continents.” These landmasses defy conventional gravitational understanding, hovering effortlessly and invisibly above the clouds, observable only during rare phenomena such as Solbrand eclipses and significant divine events.

The enigmatic properties of Ahm'a have given rise to several prominent theories regarding its nature and stability. Chief among these is the Aether-Stone Hypothesis, posited by Magister Verril in 1399 4A, suggesting that Ahm’a floats atop massive cores of arcana-stone, remnants of pre-Wrath civilizations whose magics sustain its levitation. Another influential theory is the Reversed Pulse Theory, championed by Rift Guild researchers, which speculates an inverted gravitational flow or “sky-tide” that maintains these celestial continents in perpetual suspension. Lastly, adherents to traditional teachings of the Lantern School attribute Ahm'a’s levitation to divine intervention, specifically the conscious will of Ahm, the Lantern-God.

Ahm'a is not without its own inhabitants, referred to variably in texts as the Seraphim, Skyborn, or Ahalani. Reports from fragmented sources such as Seuran skyward cults and recovered Illuminan Codices describe these beings as angelic entities capable of self-sustained flight and radiant with intense luminescence. Their societal structures, though largely speculative, indicate a rigid hierarchy built around knowledge, form, and ritual. To date, no verified envoy or emissary has successfully established prolonged contact or returned with definitive accounts, leaving open the question of whether these beings represent a genuine civilization or a manifestation of divine consciousness.

Though empirical study continues to advance our understanding, Ahm'a remains a domain deeply entrenched in mystery, compelling us to question the very fundamentals of our cosmological paradigms and inviting continued scholarly pursuit.

### **Chapter II: Limbo—The Interval Between Sky and World**

Between the celestial heights of Ahm’a and the tangible terrain of Anslem stretches an enigmatic expanse known as Limbo. Rather than existing as a distinct boundary, Limbo manifests as a chaotic atmospheric field beginning roughly three leagues above sea level and extending indefinitely upward. Explorers venturing into this unstable region have encountered numerous extraordinary phenomena that challenge conventional understanding.

Chief among Limbo's strange attributes are sudden gravitational reversals, where adventurers find themselves inexplicably inverted or suspended. The region is marked by unpredictable cyclonic air voids, invisible whirlpools capable of violently altering trajectories and threatening airships. Travelers often report experiences of profound disorientation and vivid hallucinations, potentially induced by exposure to altitude sorcery or flux-tide radiation.

The fauna of Limbo defies familiar taxonomy. Documented accounts from renowned expeditions, notably that of Sir Ven Kordall in 1387 4A, have cataloged numerous fantastical beings: riftlings, entities with ephemeral and shifting forms that blend animal instincts with the nature of air currents; featherworms, delicate creatures whose transparent membranes and bones are supported by gaseous sacs; and sky-horrors, ethereal predators with unsettling, semi-transparent anatomy.

The Monastery of Hollow Winds in southern Muraii maintains an illustrated bestiary of these Limbo inhabitants, though much remains conjectural due to the inherent dangers of field observation. Despite these uncertainties, the scientific exploration of Limbo continues to intrigue and inspire adventurous scholars, prompting deeper investigation into its mysteries and the broader implications for our cosmological understanding.

## **Chapter III: Anslem — The Surface World**

The world we inhabit—Anslem—is diverse, vibrant, and rich with life, civilization, and mystery. Our terrestrial realm forms the stable ground beneath our feet, a mosaic of lands spanning from the frozen reaches of the Shivering Wastes to the molten expanses near the southern rift zones.

### **Geographical Overview**

Anslem’s geography has been dramatically reshaped by two major historical catastrophes:

* **The Crater Event**: Occurring several years before the fall of Illuminus, an unknown force carved a vast depression known as The Crater in western Gaardia. Scholars hypothesize causes ranging from divine intervention to celestial collisions, though definitive answers elude us still.

* **The Fall of Illuminus and Formation of the Rift**: The cataclysmic descent of Illuminus, the great celestial city, created the jagged scar known as the Rift Mountains—splitting Muraii in half and forever altering trade, culture, and the continent's destiny.

### **Peoples and Cultures**

Anslem is inhabited by diverse races, each contributing uniquely to the realm’s complexity:

* **Humans** dominate through adaptability and ambition, though fractious kingdoms like Gaardia often suffer from instability and internal discord.

* **Elves** prefer seclusion, maintaining their mysteries within isolated groves like the Seclude.

* **Dwarves**, unrivaled artisans of stone and metal, dwell primarily within the Rift Mountains, forging peerless crafts and artifacts.

* **Halflings** favor simplicity, creating tightly-knit communities blending seamlessly with nature.

* **Leonin** of the northern continent, Miza, uphold honor and martial discipline within the united Mizan Empire.

* **Sea People** roam oceans, forming transient floating societies built upon great vessels, perpetually driven by tides and winds.

### **Notable Surface Phenomena**

Anslem is home to numerous phenomena directly influenced by deeper subterranean or upper-atmosphere interactions, including:

* **The Floating Spires of Rimspire**: Architectural wonders sustained by mysterious sky-rites.

* **Rift’s Breath Pulse**: A fluctuating magical energy, suggesting ongoing, invisible interactions between Limbo and subterranean realms.

## **Chapter IV: Pandemonium — Beneath Anslem’s Crust**

Beneath our feet, just below the terrestrial crust, lies Pandemonium—a vast, intricate labyrinth of caverns and tunnels defying conventional understanding.

### **Physical and Temporal Anomalies**

Pandemonium challenges our most basic perceptions of time and space. Explorers often report:

* **Temporal Loops**: Encounters where adventurers inexplicably return to their origin before departing.

* **Gravitational Anomalies**: Water flows upward, passages invert without warning.

* **Luminescent Flora**: Plants emit melodious hums and ethereal glow, casting Pandemonium in perpetual twilight.

### **Inhabitants and Mysteries**

Pandemonium hosts peculiar entities and phenomena, among them:

* **The Living Walls**: Massive structures appearing alive, rumored by dwarves to respond to emotions or memories.

* **Forgotten Roads**: Halfling tales recount mystical paths impossible to map, shifting unpredictably with each traversal.

Despite numerous expeditions, Pandemonium remains largely enigmatic, its deeper truths concealed from even the most daring explorers.

---

## **Chapter V: The Fire Deeps — The Infernal Depths**

Below Pandemonium lies a realm even more hostile—the Fire Deeps, sometimes called the True Below. A fiery abyss home to molten rivers, burning chasms, and entities drawn from nightmare itself.

### **Geography and Environment**

The Fire Deeps present a treacherous, infernal landscape:

* **Lava-Rivers and Fens**: Molten rock channels flow unpredictably, reshaping the landscape continuously.

* **Hellscapes**: Regions of intense heat, toxic fumes, and explosive eruptions.

### **Denizens: The Ifrahnir**

Dominating this realm are the Ifrahnir, or Children of the Deep Spark:

* Beings seemingly composed of flame and stone, feeding on raw heat.

* Communicating through crackling, ember-laden speech.

* Historically manifesting on the surface during forbidden rites involving bloodfire, explicitly outlawed by the Lex Gaardium since 1305 4A.

Notable historical interaction includes the Collapse of the Forge-Arch (1370 4A), attributed to an accidental breach into these fiery realms.

## **Chapter VI: Unified Models of Cosmology**

Understanding the full cosmology requires a unifying framework. Current scholarly discourse has coalesced around two predominant models:

### **The Layered Sphere Model**

Widely adopted by Rift Engineers and the Royal Mathematici, this model presents Anslem as concentric spheres:

* Innermost: The Fire Deeps

* Intermediate: Pandemonium

* Surface: Anslem’s terrestrial crust

* Above: Limbo, transitioning to celestial realms

* Uppermost: Ahm'a, the floating continents and their angelic inhabitants

This ordered structure appeals due to its alignment with observed gravitational anomalies and measured sky phenomena.

### **The Whale-Back Hypothesis**

In contrast, this older model draws from cultural myths, particularly among sea cults and certain Rimspire scholars:

* Anslem rests on the back of a divine whale swimming the cosmic Night-River.

* Ahm’a's floating continents are likened to celestial barnacles.

* Pandemonium is seen as the whale’s internal structure, and the Fire Deep its fiery belly.

While less supported by empirical evidence, the Whale-Back Hypothesis remains culturally significant, especially within folklore and certain mystical traditions.

---

## **Chapter VII: On Illuminus and the Celestial Fall**

The catastrophic event known as the Fall of Illuminus, marking the end of the 3rd Age, profoundly reshaped Anslem’s landscape and civilizations.

### **The Event and its Aftermath**

Illuminus, a once-glorious city of Ahm’a, plummeted from the skies, tearing the fabric of reality:

* Creation of the Rift Mountains, isolating Muraii’s eastern and western halves.

* Destruction of spiritual and political structures, triggering social upheaval.

* Introduction of celestial materials imbued with properties resonating both sky and subterranean magic, as revealed by recovered Illuminan fragments.

### **Impact on Civilization**

Societies across Anslem had to adapt rapidly:

* Fracturing of unified faiths, particularly the Temple of Isha.

* Emergence of new cults, kingdoms, and the reshaping of regional powers, including Gaardia’s ascension and subsequent internal strife.

The Fall of Illuminus remains a turning point in Anslem’s historical and cosmological discourse.

---

## **Chapter VIII: Reflections and Future Inquiry**

Cosmology, like Anslem itself, is dynamic. Scholars continue to probe, observe, and speculate. Critical questions remain unanswered:

* What precisely sustains Ahm'a’s levitation?

* How do the intricate gravitational anomalies interact across Pandemonium, Limbo, and the terrestrial realms?

* What further celestial influences could shape Anslem’s future?

These inquiries invite brave explorers and rigorous scholars to push ever further, reminding us that our quest to comprehend the universe is as boundless as the stars themselves.

## **Appendix A: Glossary of Terms and Concepts**

* **Ahm'a**: Celestial realm of floating continents above Anslem’s atmosphere.

* **Ahalani**: Angelic inhabitants of Ahm'a, also known as Seraphim or Skyborn.

* **Arcana-stone**: Magical stone hypothesized to sustain levitation of Ahm'a.

* **Crater Event**: Catastrophic event predating the Fall of Illuminus, resulting in the formation of the Crater.

* **Fire Deeps**: Fiery subterranean realm beneath Pandemonium, inhabited by the Ifrahnir.

* **Flux-tide Radiation**: Anomalous energy in Limbo, inducing hallucinations and disorientation.

* **Ifrahnir**: Fiery beings inhabiting the Fire Deeps, known as Children of the Deep Spark.

* **Illuminus**: Celestial city whose catastrophic fall created the Rift Mountains.

* **Limbo**: Chaotic atmospheric region between Ahm'a and Anslem, characterized by gravitational anomalies and strange fauna.

* **Pandemonium**: Complex subterranean realm directly beneath Anslem’s surface, known for temporal and gravitational anomalies.

* **Rift Mountains**: Mountain range created by the Fall of Illuminus, dividing Muraii into distinct eastern and western halves.

* **Rift-spyglass**: Advanced observational instrument enabling detailed study of celestial and atmospheric phenomena.

* **Sky-tide**: Hypothetical inverted gravitational or energetic force influencing celestial and terrestrial anomalies.

## **Appendix B: Key Figures in Cosmological Research**

* **Thaedrin Morvell**: Astronomic Philosopher of Gaardia, author of *Cosmologia Anslemica*, recognized for compiling a unified cosmological model.

* **Magister Verril**: Scholar who proposed the influential Aether-Stone Hypothesis (1399 4A), explaining Ahm’a’s levitation.

* **Sir Ven Kordall**: Noted explorer and leader of the first documented expedition into Limbo (1387 4A), significantly expanding knowledge of this chaotic region.

* **Scholar-Magus Thellenri**: Prominent Rimspire researcher known for extensive documentation of celestial phenomena and forbidden sky rites.

* **Lantern School Scholars**: Conservative religious intellectuals advocating for divine explanations of cosmological phenomena, particularly regarding Ahm'a and celestial bodies.
