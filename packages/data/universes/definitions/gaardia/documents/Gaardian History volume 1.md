### Chapter 1 – *Of Crowns and Craters: A Gaardian History from the Wrath to the Present*

*Penned by Estevan Vexmir, Royal Historian of the Court at High Gaardia, in the Year 1341 of the Fourth Age*

> *"Let not the dust of broken empires obscure the stars above, for even a cracked crown may catch the light."*
> —*Lunfried Vexmir, Chief Lexic of the Gaardian Court, 987 4A*

---

## I. INTRODUCTION

Gaardia, jewel of the western coast, has stood—scarred but steadfast—since the sundering of the Muraiian Empire. Though often beset by civil war, apostasy, and the gnawing bite of succession crises, the kingdom persists in name and in hope. The present text aims to convey a complete portrait of Gaardia as she stands in the early 14th century of the 4th Age, examining her rule, her laws, her creeds, and the men and women who have steered her course.

---

## II. ON THE ORIGINS OF THE MODERN GAARDIAN STATE

The Wrath (0 4A) broke more than temples and towers—it ended the last vestiges of Muraiian imperial unity. From the ashes rose Gaardia as a sovereign power, claiming descent from Tharian Ghondalore through his seventh great-granddaughter, **Queen Auralis the Flame-Touched** (reigned 12–63 4A). Though her rule was brief and often mythologized—particularly her survival of the Craterfire—Auralis forged the modern monarchy’s claim to divine and ancestral legitimacy.

### Notable Monarchs:

* **Queen Auralis I** “the Flame-Touched” (12–63 4A) – Founder of the modern Gaardian line.
* **King Deremond II** “the Hound of Duras” (215–237 4A) – Defeated the Corsairs of Seura; codified the Lex Gaardium.
* **Queen Iridelle IV** (871–913 4A) – Commissioned the great survey of Rimspire; presided over 40 years of relative peace.
* **Queen Meliora I** “the Pale” (1312–present) – Current ruler, ascended after the poisoning of her brother, King Ulric V.

---

## III. GOVERNANCE & THE CROWN

The Gaardian monarchy is hereditary but often contested. Rule is centered in **High Gaardia**, a fortified city of marble domes and salt-stung towers. The monarch governs with the support of a **Privy Synod**, composed of:

* The High Lexic (chief of law),
* The Exchequer of Ports,
* The Lord Marshal of the Coastal Guard,
* The Chancellor of the Crown-Faith.

Appointments are technically royal but, in truth, subject to noble pressure and mercantile bribery.

### Current Synod Members (as of 1341 4A):

* **High Lexic Yeron d’Seyne** – A strict adherent to the Lex Gaardium, recently condemned the use of divination in court trials.
* **Marshal Olvyn Bladeshore** – Commands the Coastal Guard and oversees the Duras Straits.
* **Chancellor Orvis of the Lantern Hall** – Representative of the Ahmite clergy, accused of heresy by the Rimspire Inquisition.

---

## IV. LAWS AND LEGAL CUSTOMS

Codified first under **Deremond II**, the **Lex Gaardium** remains the legal spine of Gaardia. It establishes the following key principles:

* **The King’s Peace**: Assault within city walls is a capital crime.
* **Right of Shield**: Nobles may challenge legal verdicts through ritual combat under sacred oversight.
* **Mage-Writ Prohibition**: No spellwork may be employed in court save by a licensed Arcanist of the Crown.
* **Salt-Tithe Law**: All ships docking at Gaardian ports must pay 1/16th cargo weight in salt or silver.

Legal disputes are judged by **Roving Arbiters**, itinerant magistrates who ride circuit through Rimspire, the Crater frontier, and the Wastes.

---

## V. RELIGION & THEOLOGY

Following the Wrath, the once-unified Temple of Isha fragmented. New cults and creeds took root across Gaardia’s fractured terrain.

### Major Faiths in 1341 4A:

**1. The Cult of Ahm** – Centered in Lantern Hall, High Gaardia. Views the sun-lantern as a divine intelligence.
**2. The Temple of Wilhelm** – Based in Kensir, led by the Pale See. Teaches inevitability of death and the sacred cycle of decay. Technically heretical, tolerated only by political necessity.
**3. The Church of Velis (Dreaming Lady)** – Popular among Rimspire mystics and the Seuran merchant lords. Known for visions, dreamwalking, and fatal sleep rituals.
**4. The Stone Chorus (Dwarven)** – Present in Gaardian cities with dwarven minorities. Focused on ancestral communion and memory-forging.

Clerics are licensed through the Crown-Faith Registry, though many rural cults remain unregistered.

---

## VI. FOREIGN RELATIONS

Gaardia’s diplomacy is brittle and opportunistic, its alliances shifting like Eluscion tides.

### Current Relations:

* **Duras (Vassal Duchy)** – Ostensibly loyal, but politically fractious. The Duke, **Henril Vainmor**, maintains his own fleet and ignores royal edicts.
* **Seura (City-State)** – Respected rival. Mercenaries from Seura bolster Gaardian campaigns in the Mistveil.
* **The Elves of Ghond** – Keep formal neutrality. Rumors persist that **Speaker Vaeril Aerendas** of Ghond has gifted Meliora a charm of Seclude origin—potentially illegal under the Crown-Wardens’ edicts.
* **Farsreach** – Profitable but politically aloof. Its **Tower-Senate** denies Gaardian taxes, citing pre-Wrath independence.

---

## VII. GEOGRAPHY AND INFRASTRUCTURE

Gaardia spans coast, ruin, and rim. Its roads remain fractured, and many trade routes are guarded not by knights but hired caravaneers.

### Key Locations:

* **The Crater** – Still radioactive to spellwork. Used as exile territory for rogue casters.
* **Rimspire** – Center of forbidden lore and sky-rites. Ruled by the eccentric **Arch-Inquirer Vos Fenriel**.
* **Firelight** – Small mysterious village; site of the 1289 mass disappearance.
* **Veilstone** – Southernmost town; vital for winter wheat and cold-forging.
* **Duras** – Northern port city; controls most salt and fish trade.

---

## VIII. NOTABLE FIGURES OF THE PRESENT ERA

* **Queen Meliora I** – Frail in health, iron in will. Known for restoring the fractured east road to Ghond.
* **Sir Varin Blackscale** – Knight-Captain of the Riftwardens. Once led the purge of the Mistfen Cult in 1324.
* **Scholar-Magus Thellenri of Rimspire** – Author of *Stars That Weep*, banned by Lantern Hall.
* **Mistress Ythra Nyneve** – Alleged necromancer; last seen passing into the Crater under Seuran escort.

---

## IX. CULTURE, ART, AND FOLKWAYS

Despite turmoil, Gaardia remains a wellspring of storytelling and tradition.

* **Midskald** remains the greatest festival, marked by fires, salted fruit, and dream feasts.
* **The Silver Thread** is the national epic, chronicling the fall of Illuminus and the exile of Tharian’s heirs.
* **Gloomglass** from the Mistveil coast is prized in crafts; said to hum when held by the faithful.

Music, prophecy, and wrestling remain the dominant peasant pastimes, particularly during Frostmoot.

## X. ON THE HOLIDAYS & FESTIVALS OF GAARDIA

*“Let no throne stand so proud as to forget the hearth, nor any sword so sharp it cannot dance.”*
—Old Gaardian proverb, recited each New Cycle

### Overview

Though fractured in faith and politics, the Kingdom of Gaardia remains rich in ritual. Festivals serve not only as religious observance but also as vital threads stitching together scattered peoples, reminding even the Crater-bound or ice-lost that they share a common wheel of seasons. The Gaardian calendar—established during the early reign of Tharian Ghondalore and revised post-Wrath—recognizes ten months of thirty days, each imbued with historic or spiritual resonance.

---

### 1. **Frostmoot (Month I)**

**Major Festival: *The Binding Hearth***

* **Date:** 11–15 Frostmoot
* **Significance:** Midwinter rite of familial bond and survival.
* **Rituals:** Hearthfires are lit in silence at sunset. Elders bind red thread around the wrists of children for health. Common folk gather to share preserved food and recite "The First Snow Prayer" to Ahm.
* **Regional Notes:** In Veilstone, bonfires are kindled on sea-ice, and salted fish is thrown into the flames “to trick the hunger spirits.”

---

### 2. **Runvakr (Month II)**

**Major Festival: *Tharian’s Oath***

* **Date:** 12 Runvakr (First Day of Spring)
* **Significance:** Celebration of the unification of early Muraii.
* **Rituals:** Oath reenactments are performed in public squares, and children swear loyalty to “stone, sun, and sovereign.”
* **Scholarly Note:** Many scholars believe the date of Tharian’s oath was retroactively aligned with the equinox for political reasons under Queen Auralis I.

---

### 3. **Stormvakt (Month III)**

\*\*Lesser Festival: *The Craterwatch*

* **Date:** Variable (based on the lunar shadowfall over the Crater)
* **Significance:** Mourning and observance of the Wrath Event.
* **Rituals:** In Rimspire, white veils are worn, and no iron is carried for a day. In High Gaardia, Crown mages perform a protective warding.
* **Controversy:** The Pale See of Kensir condemns the festival as a “vain memorial of failure.”

---

### 4. **Austursól (Month IV)**

**Major Festival: *The Flowering Sun***

* **Date:** 21 Austursól (First Day of Summer)
* **Significance:** Celebration of Ahm’s full strength and divine favor.
* **Rituals:** Sun-lanterns are raised across rooftops; children paint prayers on seashells, which are cast into the Eluscion Sea.
* **Noble Tradition:** Gaardian knights host public tournaments, including archery and wrestle-circle duels.

---

### 5. **Solbrand (Month V)**

**Lesser Festival: *The Salt Revels***

* **Date:** 9–10 Solbrand
* **Significance:** Merchant holiday tied to the old salt tithe.
* **Rituals:** Salted breads are shared among port cities. Marketkeepers wear blue and silver sashes.
* **Duras Practice:** A parade of scale-bearers walks the port, carrying carved ledgers of past debts repaid.

---

### 6. **Midskald (Month VI)**

\*\*Major Festival: *High Midsummer*

* **Date:** 1 Midskald
* **Significance:** Apex of summer, celebration of song, light, and vitality.
* **Rituals:** Story-singers travel between hamlets, exchanging verse for bread and coin. A Dreamfast is held for the goddess Velis: the faithful sleep under moonlight to receive visions.
* **Royal Custom:** The monarch appears in public in a silver cloak and recites a verse from *The Silver Thread*, the national epic.

---

### 7. **Haflund (Month VII)**

**Lesser Festival: *The Breaking of the First Leaf***

* **Date:** 24 Haflund (First Day of Fall)
* **Significance:** A farming holiday marking the end of summer yields.
* **Rituals:** Children collect “first-fall” leaves and press them in books. Offerings of bread and bone are buried beneath trees.
* **Halfling Influence:** The Hearthroot Hollows’ tradition of “seed-cakes and stories” has been adopted widely in the Gaardian south.

---

### 8. **Álfsól (Month VIII)**

\*\*Lesser Festival: *Veil-Kissing*

* **Date:** 17 Álfsól
* **Significance:** A romantic and mystical observance honoring the unseen.
* **Rituals:** Couples exchange woven charms in secret. Offerings of milk and herbs are left in hollow trees.
* **Elven Origin:** Originally an elvish twilight rite, this festival was adopted during the reign of Queen Iridelle IV, who allegedly held elven blood.

---

### 9. **Skjaldsfang (Month IX)**

\*\*Major Festival: *The Grey Vigil*

* **Date:** 28 Skjaldsfang (First Day of Winter)
* **Significance:** Day of remembrance for the dead.
* **Rituals:** Silent processions carry bone lanterns through city streets. Offerings of ash, salt, and wool are placed in ancestral cairns.
* **Wilhelm’s Influence:** Though not officially endorsed, many now include prayers to the god of death. Crown clerics walk a fine line to avoid offending the Pale See.

---

### 10. **Vetrmyrk (Month X)**

\*\*Grand Festival: *New Cycle Feast (Turn-Eve)*

* **Date:** 30 Vetrmyrk
* **Significance:** Year’s end and the start of the new. A time of forgiveness, revelry, and divination.
* **Rituals:** Wine is poured on soil at midnight “to awaken the seed.” A child dressed in white is carried on a cart through villages as the embodiment of the new year.
* **Common Rhyme:**
  *“Bless the black and bless the white,
  Sleep the old and rise the light.”*

---

### Concluding Note

These festivals form the beating heart of Gaardian life. They span from noble halls to peasant hearths, from mountain stone to Crater ash. Though customs vary across region and caste, each celebration draws Gaardians back to the rhythm of the land, the stars, and the stories that endure even when empires do not.

> *“We are not kept by the crown, but by the calendar.”*
> —Ythra Tallowmere, Sage of Farsreach, 1298 4A
