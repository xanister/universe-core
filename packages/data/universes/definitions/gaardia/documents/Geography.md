## **🏔 Anslem Geography & Major Regions**

### **🌍 The Continent of Muraii**

**Once unified under a mighty imperial banner, Muraii now stands fractured—a land torn by the Rift Mountains and sundered by the perilous Eluscion Sea.**

#### **Western Muraii**

* **Gaardia: A human kingdom mired in courtly intrigues and shaky claims of descent from the legendary Tharian Ghondalore. Endless succession crises erode its stability.**

* **The Crater: An enormous depression scarring western Gaardia, born of an ancient, poorly understood catastrophe sometime near the end of the 3rd age. Local legends abound, each region offering a different cause—divine wrath, fallen star, or cursed magic.** 

* **Rimspire: A daring city built along the Crater’s very rim, trading on its precarious fame. Western most city in Gaardia.**

* **Duras: A thriving trade hub on the northern coast, serving as a conduit for goods from inland and across the Eluscion Sea. Northernmost city in Gaardia.**

* **Kensir: A blighted land haunted by spectral terrors on the western coast north of Farsreach, secretly ruled by an undead kingdom. Few outsiders enter, fewer still emerge unchanged. Known as the Aeon Sovereignty.** 

* **Farsreach: A prosperous independent coastal city-state in the southwest, overshadowed by the titanic ruins of a tower that once scraped the very heavens.**

#### **The Eluscion Sea**

* **The Eluscion Sea: A vast, dangerous bay that almost cuts Muraii in half from the north. It is notorious for shipwrecks and piracy. Its scattered isles and turbulent currents defy organized naval power.**

* **Gaardia (City): Capital of the Gaardian kingdom, perched on the western coast of the Eluscion sea and in the far east of the country of Gaardia.**

* **Seura: A proud, independent city-state south of the city of Gaardia, safeguarding its autonomy through careful diplomacy and mercenary strength.**

* **Ghond: An ancient eastern elven coastal city, governed by merchant oligarchs whose fortunes rise on ocean trade.**

* **Firelight: A modest village atop the ruins of an old fortress, whispering with ghost stories along the northwestern shores.**  
* **Taurengard: Also known as Drakaar. Fishing village built on the ruins of an ancient city fabled to be inhabited by minotaurs. On Southern coast of the Eluscion sea where the Kurn river meets the sea.** 

#### **South Muraii**

* **Rift Mountains: A formidable barrier that chokes trade and divides Muraii east from west culturally and economically.**

* **Hearthhome: A dwarven stronghold burrowed deep into these mountains, famed for its peerless mines and artistry in metal and gem.**

* **Thornroot Forest: A sprawling wood teeming with life, holding:**

  * **Hearthroot Hollows: Peaceful halfling communities living in snug, well-tended burrows, seamlessly blending with the woodland.**  
      
* **The Shivering Wastes: A cold, unrelenting tundra east of the Rift where survival demands hardiness.**

* **Mistveil Moors: Fog-shrouded marshes near the southern coast and west of the Rift.**

  * **Fogfen Cross: A crucial hamlet where traders brave the mists.**

  * **Shadowwatch Keep: A lonely military outpost ever vigilant for lurking threats.**

  * **Mistharbor: A precarious trading village, its docks often cloaked in eerie gloom.**

  * **Greenmire Clusters: Hardy farm settlements wringing life from cold soil.**

  * **Veilstone: The largest local town, serving as the administrative and cultural heart of the Wastes.**

### **🌎 The Continent of Miza**

**Far to the north of Muraii sprawls Miza, Anslem’s largest continent, a realm of striking contrasts.**

#### **The Mizan Desert (Northern Miza)**

* **The Mizan Empire: Dominated by disciplined Leonin clans along the life-giving Thebian River. Once enslaved under the sorcerous Rashakas, who fell in 520 of the 3rd Age. By 812, the Leonin had fully unified the desert sands, forging a civilization rooted in honor and martial tradition.**

#### **The Tharian Jungle (Southern Miza)**

* **New Tharia: Once a formidable human kingdom whose might rivaled any empire, now reduced to scattered fishing villages swallowed by lush jungle, their grandeur lost to vine and memory.**
