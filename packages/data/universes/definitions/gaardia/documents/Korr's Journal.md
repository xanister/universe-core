### **📘 *Excerpts from the Hidden Journal of Korr Brightblade***

*Scratched into smuggled parchment using charcoal and rust, bound in stitched thread from torn bedding.*  
 **Recovered post-escape from a hidden cache in a vent behind Level III, Cell 19\.**

---

**Entry – 14.08.1465 4A**  
 *First month. The cold has shape here.*  
 I’ve fought beasts, soldiers, even a stone-skinned ogre once—but none of them pressed into the bones like this frost. It creeps, not just into the body, but into thought. Discipline is my shield. I rise, stretch, drill footwork in the dark when I can. I recite names—mine, Sefton’s, Rockwell’s. Every morning. It helps.  
 The guards don’t speak unless it’s to strike. Food is half-frozen starch and something oily. Might be fish. Or rats.  
 There’s a vent behind the bench. It wheezes at night. Like breath.

---

**Entry – 01.10.1465 4A**  
 *I saw a man whisper to a stone today. Kissed it, too.*  
 They say this place has five levels. I’m on the third. No one talks about the fifth.  
 Bren says the frost on the walls listens. He tells me to be careful what I think.  
 He also named a moth and held a funeral for a mushroom last week.  
 I think he was a priest once. Or maybe he just dreams of fire.

They moved Rockwell and Sefton after intake. I hear nothing. Maybe that’s better. I can’t stop thinking about Blackfont. Richter's voice, steady as ever, while we were buried in blood.

He knew what he was doing. He knew what they’d say we did.

---

**Entry – 17.12.1465 4A**  
 *A man died in the gauntlet yesterday. The warden laughed.*  
 Tembryk made two boys fight for water. Said whoever bled first would get a cup.  
 Neither did.  
 They both froze that night.  
 I told myself once I wouldn’t let this place change me. I still wake before the torch passes. I still drill. But I don’t stand straight after. My spine’s starting to curve inward. Like it’s hiding from something.  
 I found a sliver of glass near the privy trench. Kept it. Could be useful. Could be an end.  
 But not yet. I have work unfinished.

---

**Entry – 04.02.1466 4A**  
 *Sefton’s alive. I saw him.*  
 Two guards dragging him past the ash corridor. He looked smaller. Leaner. But his eyes—still sharp. He didn’t look at me. I don’t know if he saw. Maybe he couldn’t risk it.  
 Rockwell’s name hasn’t passed anyone’s lips.  
 They say the lowest level floods now and then. Steam melts ice, then the frost comes again. Bodies get caught in the cycle. Half-frozen, half-boiled. They don’t even open the doors down there anymore.  
 We’re ghosts already.  
 I recited the names again. This time, I added one:  
 **Richter.**  
 Not out of honor.  
 Out of promise.

---

**Entry – 23.09.1466 4A**  
 *I found the gap.*  
 The vent behind the Ashgate pipe—warm enough to melt the ice if blocked just right.  
 It hisses when it breathes. A sound like a whistle. I timed it with the night bell. The guards don’t notice—it’s always wheezing.  
 Bren helped. Said he had no interest in leaving, but liked the idea that someone might.  
 I left him half the food I’ve hoarded.  
 Tomorrow, I crawl. Through the warmth. Through the dark.  
 If I don’t make it, I’ll rot with dignity.  
 If I do—  
 …I bring the storm back with me.

---

Entry \- 24.09.1466 4A — Icehold, outer louvre ledge (east face, Sluice Four)

I made it to the ledge. Wind gnaws through the slats and leaves salt on the tongue. The louvre shudders when the works breathe; frost blooms along the iron like white mold. I’ve wedged myself into the lee of the frame to keep from being blown off the ledge. The skin under my beard is numb. Good. Numb forgets.

Istra sits beside me, back to the stone, knees to her chest, eyes on the dark. She wears the prison in the way she holds herself—ready to flinch, ready to run—but her hands know these vents. She traced the route here with three quick lines in dust on my boot and never once looked lost. 

I can still smell the bowels of the place on my clothes—old brine, rust, and that dead thing below that groped by sound more than sight. I left it angry and hungry in the dark. Dropped my pipe in the scramble; it clanged like a bell and bought us seconds. I’ll thank that scrap of iron if I live long enough to be superstitious again.
