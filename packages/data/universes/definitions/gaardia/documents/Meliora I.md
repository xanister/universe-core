# **Meliora I “the Pale”**

*Born 1284 4A – Crowned 1312 4A*
*By Order of the Royal Scribes, Lantern Hall Archives, High Gaardia*

---

> *“Her hands are pale as moonstone, yet they bear the weight of iron.”*
> —Marshal Olvyn Bladeshore, Privy Synod address, 1321 4A

---

### **Early Life**

Meliora, second child of King Altherion III and Queen Saerwyn of the Duras line, was born in the Year 1284 of the Fourth Age within the salt-stung halls of High Gaardia’s coastal palace. Her youth was marked by frail health, a pallor that earned her the name “Pale Child” among court servants. Despite her constitution, she received rigorous instruction in the Lex Gaardium, statecraft, and the tangled histories of Tharianic descent.

She spent much of her adolescence in the company of High Lexic tutors and the Stone Chorus dwarves resident in High Gaardia, fostering an affinity for legal precedent and the austere, ancestral rites of the dwarves.

---

### **Path to the Throne**

In 1312 4A, tragedy struck: her elder brother, King Ulric V, died suddenly from poisoning at a feast held in the Hall of Salt. The Crown’s official proclamation named the cause as “corrupted wine from Seura,” though many in the Synod whispered of internal conspiracy. Meliora’s succession was contested by Duke Henril Vainmor of Duras, who claimed the throne under a distant blood tie, but her right was upheld by the Privy Synod through invocation of the Grace of Inheritance.

Her coronation was held on the Day of the Grey Vigil, the first monarch to take the oath beneath bone lanterns since Queen Iridelle IV.

---

### **Reign**

Meliora’s rule has been defined by resilience in the face of political fracture. She restored the east road to Ghond, reopening trade with the elves despite Crown-Wardens’ objections. Her court is an uneasy alliance of coastal merchants, Rimspire mystics, and Lantern Hall clergy.

**Key policies and events:**

* **The East Road Reclamation (1318 4A):** Repaired after decades of neglect, fostering renewed contact with Ghond and Rimspire.
* **Suppression of the Mistfen Cult Remnants (1324 4A):** Delegated to Sir Varin Blackscale, ending years of guerilla harassment along the Mistveil coast.
* **The Quiet Salt War (1327–1331 4A):** A trade standoff with Duras, ended through the Salt Revels Concord, though tensions persist.

---

### **Personality and Court**

Despite her title, Meliora’s pallor belies an unyielding will. She is known for speaking little in public session, preferring pointed questions to speeches, and often allowing rivals to entangle themselves in their own rhetoric before passing judgment.

Her detractors claim she “rules from the shadow of her mother’s counsel,” while her allies credit her with holding Gaardia together in an age when the realm might otherwise have fractured into city-states.

The queen is rarely seen without her silvered lantern of Ahm, said to have been a gift from Speaker Vaeril Aerendas of Ghond — a charm of Seclude origin, possibly illegal under Crown-Wardens’ edicts.

---

### **Legacy in Motion**

As of this writing (1341 4A), Queen Meliora remains upon the throne, her health still delicate but her authority unbroken. Whether her reign will herald the strengthening of Gaardia or its slow dissolution will be determined in the years to come.
