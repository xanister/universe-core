# **📜 Peoples & Cultures of Anslem**

## **I. The Peoples of Anslem**

### **Humans**

**Humans are the most populous of Anslem’s races, renowned for their extraordinary adaptability. This very trait fuels both their rise and perpetual discord; ambition and fear often splinter their societies, leading to fractious kingdoms and endless succession crises.**

### **Elves**

**Long-lived and elusive, elves dwell in secluded groves and hidden sanctuaries. They hold themselves as guardians of delicate natural balances, withdrawing deeper into secrecy whenever mortal strife grows too loud. Their retreat during the 3rd Age to form what is simply known as the Seclude stands as testament to their preference for isolation over turmoil.**

### **Dwarves**

**Masters of stone and forge, dwarves carve their realms deep within the Rift Mountains. Their halls echo with the steady cadence of hammer on anvil, and their unsurpassed craft in metal and gemwork remains a cornerstone of trade across the continent.**

### **Halflings**

**Cheerful and agrarian by nature, halflings live in harmonious clusters such as the Hearthroot Hollows within the Thornroot Forest. Their culture celebrates close kinship, warm hospitality, and a simple yet contented life, often blending so seamlessly with their woodlands that strangers may pass through unaware of the burrows around them.**

### **Leonin**

**Fierce and disciplined, the leonine tribes of the north coalesced into the Mizan Empire, a society ruled by rigorous codes of honor and law. Their legacy is one of martial pride, forged through overthrowing their old oppressors and uniting the sands of Miza under a single banner by 812 of the 3rd Age.**

### **Sea People**

**These enigmatic mariners roam the depths upon enormous vessels that, over time, cluster into temporary floating cities. Their societies exist in constant flux, breaking apart after years to drift once more across the oceans, sustaining a life defined by tides and salt.**

## **II. Magic & Mysticism**

**Magic in Anslem is a subtle, often perilous current running through every culture. Though wondrous in potential, it is capricious and rare, and its practitioners tread a precarious line between awe and suspicion. Folklore across every people brims with cautionary tales, tragic songs, and whispered legends of spells gone awry—an ever-present reminder that power, untamed, courts ruin.**

---

## **III. Faiths & Hidden Powers**

**The 4th Age witnesses a profusion of religions and fervent cults. Among the foremost divine figures are:**

* **Anslem, revered by druids and elves as the living embodiment of the world itself.**

* **Ahm, the radiant sun, worshipped as the source of harvest and renewal.**

* **Isha, goddess of magic and fire, whose temples once safeguarded ancient arcana before many fell into ruin.**

* **Thakash, once a mortal hero, now elevated by legend into near-divinity.**

* **Visith, dread goddess of winter and domination, invoked in curses and stories meant to cow the young.**

* **Wilhelm (also Morvael), the stern god of death and time, solemnly honored by morticians and quietly courted by necromancers.**

* **Velis (also Lethielle or Drimaris), goddess of dreams, blamed equally for nightmares and mysterious deaths in sleep.**

## **V. The March of Ages**

### **The 1st Age (0–1121)**

* **0: Tharian Ghondalore unites Muraii under the imperial crown.**

* **15: Outbreak of Isha’s War, equal parts holy crusade and territorial conquest.**

* **26: Isha Temples completed to house arcane lore.**

* **1121: Age closes under growing strains.**

  ### **The 2nd Age (0–3912)**

* **0: Death of the last Muraiian Emperor fractures the empire.**

* **10: The Watcher Order arises to protect Isha’s sacred sites.**

* **3912: A slow decline brings the age to its close.**

  ### **The 3rd Age (0–1526)**

* **0: Fall of Illuminus, shaking spiritual and political orders.**

* **2: The Fold forms to guard eastern Muraii from undead threats.**  
* **26: Wilhelm founds his kingdom on ruins of the old city of Kensir**

* **520: Collapse of the first Mizan Empire.**

* **525: Yo’Tharian Civil War ravages the west.**

* **720: Elves withdraw into the Seclude.**

* **812: Leonin unify Miza, founding a renewed Mizan Empire.**

* **1520: Gaardian Civil War disrupts the west.**

* **1526: Age concludes in turbulence.**

  ### **The 4th Age (0–Present)**

* **0: The Wrath Event destroys much of civilization, obliterating most Isha Temples.**

* **220: Final stronghold of The Fold falls.**

* **262: William of the Grasses rises, reshaping regional powers.**

* **1472: The current year.**
