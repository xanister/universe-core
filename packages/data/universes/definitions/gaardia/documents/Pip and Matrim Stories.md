***Late Summer, 1432 4A — the back lanes of Hearthhome***

“Bet you can’t hop the tannery wall,” Pip hissed, copper-blond hair sticking up like straw.

Matrim’s eyes narrowed. “I *could*. The question is: what happens **after**?”

“After,” Pip said, “we pocket one of Master Colvin’s sky-blue dye cakes and *run*. I hear they burst if you drop ’em in water—turn the whole pool turquoise.”

“That sounds… magnificent.” Matrim cracked a grin. “Also irresponsible.”

“Magnificently irresponsible,” Pip corrected, and vaulted the wall.

Matrim followed, quieter but no less nimble. Inside, the tannery yard steamed with vats. Bars of compressed pigment sat drying on a rack.

Pip pointed. “Treasure.”

“Oi\!” boomed a voice. Colvin’s journeyman, a burly dwarf named Harrick, strode out, apron flapping. “Who’s skulking in my yard?”

“Improvisation time,” Matrim whispered.

Pip swept up a broom, bowed theatrically. “Good evening, sir\! The Guild sent us to test… uh… *broom handle durability*.”

Matrim’s jaw dropped. Harrick’s brows did likewise. “Broom… handle… what?”

“Allow me to demonstrate\!” Pip twirled the broom like a quarterstaff—*crack\!*—and *accidentally* knocked three dye cakes off the rack. They hit a rinse vat with satisfying *plunks*. Turquoise foam erupted, sloshing over the rim and racing across the cobbles toward Harrick’s boots.

Harrick bellowed, slipped, and disappeared in a wave of vividly blue suds.

“Run\!” Pip yelled, but Matrim seized his sleeve.

“Not past him—we’ll be cornered at the gate. Up the scaffolding\!”

They clambered the wooden frame, dye-water licking the planks beneath. Harrick surfaced, equal parts fury and aquamarine.

“You little otter-spawn\!”

“At least he’s colorful,” Pip puffed.

The scaffolding led to a linen-drying line stretched toward the alley. Pip balanced across first.

Matrim hesitated, thirty feet of wobbling rope beneath. “Why do I let you talk me into—”

“Because you *love* it\!” Pip called.

Matrim exhaled, stepped onto the line—just as a gust billowed the linens. He swayed, arms pinwheeling.

“Mat, *focus*\!” Pip shouted—and flung the broom like a counterweight. Matrim caught it, regained balance, and in three hurried strides joined Pip on the far roof.

Below, Harrick slogged through turquoise sludge, shaking a fist. “I’m sending the watch after you terror-whelps\!”

Pip offered a victorious whoop. Matrim just laughed—breathless, half-terrified, wholly alive.

They tumbled onto the tiles, side by side.

“Next time,” Matrim said between breaths, “we steal something *less messy*. Like a dragon.”

Pip grinned. “Dream big or stay home, brother.”

A watch bell clanged in the distance. Together, they scrambled off the roof and vanished into the dusk—two boys, one broom, and a story the tanners of Hearthhome would be telling for years.
